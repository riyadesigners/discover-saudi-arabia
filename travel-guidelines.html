to Saudi Arabia from IndiaTravel guidelines

Planning on exploring the Arabian Kingdom’s history, culture and adventurous terrains? Here are a few travel guidelines to Saudi Arabia from India to make your travel hassle-free.

Be Informed The Saudi government keeps providing the latest information regarding travel to and within Saudi. Following these guidelines will be beneficial for all travellers. Download the Saudi Ministry of Health’s dedicated mobile app, Mawid, which offers current healthcare information. There is a hotline available at 937. For the most recent news on travel policies, visit the ministry’s website.

Is it safe to travel to Saudi Arabia? Yes, absolutely. Saudi Arabia is safe for male as well as female travellers. Saudi welcomes tourists from all over the world. Like any other country in the world, travellers need to be respectful and adhere to local customs and laws. Ensure that you consult travel advisories before your trip.

Currency Exchange Almost all Saudi banks offer currency exchange services, which makes it convenient to exchange Indian currency in Saudi. Exchange centres are located at airports, shopping centres, etc. Even ATMs are widely available across the country..

Code & Etiquette Guidelines Depending on the establishment, the dress code may be casual or sophisticated. Visiting places of worship specifically requires you to dress modestly. Bad language and loud behaviour are not acceptable in the country. It is advised to maintain public decorum at all times.

Alcohol & Substances It’s illegal in Saudi Arabia to sell, purchase and consume alcohol and drugs.

Emergency Numbers
Below is the list of a list of emergency contact numbers in Saudi Arabia.
  • Police
    • Makkah, Riyadh & Eastern provinces: 911
    • All other provinces of the Kingdom: 999
  • Saudi Ambulance: 997
  • Civil Defense: 998
  • For traffic accidents: 993
  • Highway Patrol: 996
  • Border Guard: 994
